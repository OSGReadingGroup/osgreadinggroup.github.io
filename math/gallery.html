\[ \large \mathrm{HHH}(\beta) \propto \bigoplus_i \mathrm{gr}_\ast^\mathsf{W}\, \mathrm{H}_{c, G}^\ast(\mathcal{Z}(\beta))[\Lambda^i] \]

Above,
  • \(\beta\) is an element of the positive braid monoid of the Weyl group \(W\) of a reductive group \(G\) over the algebraic closure of a finite field;
  • \(\mathrm{HHH}\) is its triply-graded Khovanov–Rozansky homology;
  • \(\mathcal{Z}(\beta)\) is what I call its Steinberg variety;
  • \(\mathrm{H}_{c, G}^\ast\) denotes compactly-supported \(G\)-equivariant \(\ell\)-adic cohomology;
  • \(\mathsf{W}\) is the weight filtration on the cohomology; and
  • \([\Lambda^i]\) denotes the \(\Lambda^i\)-isotypic part of a representation of \(W\), where \(\Lambda^i\) is the \(i\)th exterior power of the reflection representation.

The right-hand side is thus equipped with three gradings—cohomological, weight, and isotypic—and the symbol \(\propto\) asserts that these match the triple grading on the left-hand side up to shifts and normalizations.
